🌍 About NASA & Space Exploration

NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration) is the United States government agency responsible for the nation’s civilian space program and for aeronautics and aerospace research. Since its founding in 1958, NASA has led remarkable missions—from landing humans on the Moon to exploring distant planets with robotic spacecraft. Space exploration helps us discover the origins of our universe, understand our own planet better, and inspires future generations.

🌌 Milestones in Space

~ 1958: NASA was founded, marking the beginning of America’s official journey into space exploration and scientific discovery.🧑‍🔬

~ 1969: Apollo 11 successfully landed astronauts on the Moon,🌕 making Neil Armstrong the first human to step onto another world.

~ 1990: The Hubble Space Telescope was launched,🔭 providing breathtaking images and transforming our understanding of the universe.

~ 2021: The James Webb Space Telescope was launched 🚀, opening a new era of astronomy with its powerful infrared vision.
